The Big Hack of 2011 was the first hackathon at which I was able to plan, execute, and completely finish a project along with teammates. Our project, Thunder Ant, was a way to visualize data. More specifically, we took the sequence of integers and did the following:

  1. Imagine a (Thunder) ant that knows how to count integers.
  2. For each integer, if the number is prime, turn left.
  3. Otherwise, go straight.
These three simple rules allow the ant to trace an absolutely incredible path in the two-dimensional plane, creating an almost fractal-esque image with both very dense neighborhoods and very sparse neighborhoods. If this same concept could be applied to other sequences, there may likely be other very interesting patterns.
