The scheme interpreter was my final project for CS61A, the introductory course to programming (in Python/Scheme). In its essence, we wrote an interpreter for Scheme, a dialect of Lisp, in Python. Even though, at this point, I was very familiar with Python and how to organize my projects, the rather rigid structure we were given caused my partner, Michael, and I many a headache. There were also a few interesting algorithmic problems we had to complete in Scheme. All in all, this project demonstrated our budding mastery of Python.
